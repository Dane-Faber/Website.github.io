Harley Davidson Facts

Harley-Davidson is a legendary American motorcycle manufacturer that was founded in 1903. One of Harley-Davidson's most notable features is its V-twin engine design, which provides a distinctive sound and a unique riding experience. The company has a strong heritage and culture, with many riders and enthusiasts considering themselves part of the Harley-Davidson family. Harley-Davidson is also known for its cruiser-style motorcycles, which offer a comfortable riding position and a relaxed, laid-back feel. The company has produced many iconic models over the years, including the Sportster, Softail, and Touring lines. Harley-Davidson has also been involved in racing, both on the flat track and in drag racing, and has a history of customization and personalization, with many riders customizing their bikes to make them their own. Overall, Harley-Davidson motorcycles are known for their iconic design, unique sound, and strong community of riders and enthusiasts.
